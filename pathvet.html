Infectious · Clostridial · Ovine
Enterotoxemia
(Pulpy Kidney Disease)
Clostridium perfringens type D · Small Intestine, Kidney, Brain · Ovine > Caprine

A peracute to acute toxaemia caused by the proliferation of Clostridium perfringens type D in the small intestine and subsequent absorption of epsilon toxin. Primarily affects well-nourished young lambs on high-grain or lush pasture. Gross findings include soft, autolytic kidneys and watery intestinal contents; histopathology reveals bilateral symmetric encephalomalacia in subacute survivors.

Gross Pathology Histopathology Differential Diagnosis Zoonotic: No Notifiable: No
Mortality High Onset Peracute
