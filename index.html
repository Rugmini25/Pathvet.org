Featured Disease Entry

Enterotoxemia

Pulpy Kidney Disease · Overeating Disease

Ovine · Caprine Infectious — Clostridial GI · Nervous System Notifiable in some regions

Caused by Clostridium perfringens type D, enterotoxemia is a peracute to acute disease of sheep and goats characterized by overgrowth of the organism in the small intestine and elaboration of epsilon toxin. The toxin increases intestinal and vascular permeability, leading to systemic intoxication and rapid death — often without premonitory signs.

Read Full Entry
Key Gross Findings
  • Kidneys Bilateral softening and autolysis — "pulpy" consistency on cut section; cortical pallor
  • Small intestine Hemorrhagic enteritis; watery, blood-tinged contents; mucosal congestion
  • Pericardial sac Fibrinous pericarditis; straw-colored excess pericardial fluid
  • Brain Focal symmetrical encephalomalacia in peracute survivors
  • Blood glucose Glucosuria and hyperglycemia at necropsy (ante-mortem indicator)
